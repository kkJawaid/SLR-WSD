# Research Questions

## PCC (Population, Concept, Context) Mapping

PRISMA 2020 supports PICO, PECO, PCC, and other question frameworks
(item 4). This review uses the PCC framework, which is appropriate for
methodological/architectural reviews where there is no comparator group.

| Element | Specification |
|---|---|
| **Population (P)** | large language model, LLM, fine-tuning, machine learning, transformer, Alif model, LLaMA |
| **Concept (C)** | word sense disambiguation, WSD, lexical ambiguity, knowledge-based system, knowledge-augmented, structured lexical resources, WordNet, linguistic corpora, semantic understanding |
| **Context (C)** | Urdu, low-resource language, NLP, natural language processing, reading assistance, context-sensitive vocabulary |

## Research Questions

**RQ1.** What machine learning techniques and knowledge representation paradigms (e.g.,
ontologies, knowledge graphs, rule-based logic) are currently utilized for Word Sense
Disambiguation in Urdu and comparable low-resource languages?

**RQ2.** What are the prevalent architectural patterns, reasoning flows, and system com-
ponents defining existing Knowledge-Based Systems in the NLP domain?

**RQ3.** How are structured lexical datasets, specifically WordNet and linguistic corpora,
integrated with underlying models (such as LLMs) to augment knowledge and inform
reasoning pipelines?

**RQ4.** What are the primary limitations and research gaps in current WSD systems
regarding fragmented architectures, ad-hoc dataset integration, and a lack of explainabil-
ity?

## Justification of question framework choice

PCC (rather than PICO) is used because research into Knowledge-Based Systems 
and LLM fine-tuning for low-resource languages is fundamentally methodological — 
primary studies present new architectures, integration strategies, and evaluation 
frameworks, rather than comparing two clinical interventions on a defined population. 
This follows PRISMA 2020 item 4 which permits alternative frameworks where 
appropriate to the review type.
